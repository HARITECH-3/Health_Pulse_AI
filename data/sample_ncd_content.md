# Sample NCD Content

This file contains sample content about Non-Communicable Diseases that can be used to populate the knowledge base or for testing purposes.

## Diabetes Mellitus

### Overview
Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when the pancreas is no longer able to make insulin, or when the body cannot make good use of the insulin it produces.

### Types
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: Autoimmune condition where the body attacks insulin-producing cells
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: Most common form, where the body becomes resistant to insulin
- **Gestational Diabetes**: Develops during pregnancy

### Key Statistics
- 422 million people worldwide have diabetes
- 1.5 million deaths are directly attributed to diabetes each year
- Most common in low and middle-income countries

## Cardiovascular Diseases

### Overview
Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are disorders of the heart and blood vessels, including coronary heart disease, cerebrovascular disease, and peripheral arterial disease.

### Common Conditions
- **Coronary Artery Disease**: Narrowing of heart arteries
- **Stroke**: Interruption of blood flow to brain
- **Heart Failure**: Heart cannot pump blood effectively
- **Hypertension**: High blood pressure

### Risk Factors
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Physical inactivity

## Cancer

### Overview
Cancer is a disease characterized by uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells.

### Common Types
- **Lung Cancer**: Leading cause of cancer death
- **Breast Cancer**: Most common cancer in women
- **Colorectal Cancer**: Third most common cancer worldwide
- **Prostate Cancer**: Most common cancer in men

### Warning Signs
- Unexplained weight loss
- Persistent fatigue
- Changes in bowel or bladder habits
- Unusual bleeding or discharge
- Persistent cough or hoarseness

## Chronic Respiratory Diseases

### Overview
Chronic respiratory diseases are diseases of the airways and other structures of the lungs, including asthma and COPD.

### Major Conditions
- **Asthma**: Chronic inflammation of airways
- **COPD**: Progressive lung disease including emphysema and chronic bronchitis
- **Pulmonary Fibrosis**: Scarring of lung tissue

### Environmental Factors
- Tobacco smoke
- Air pollution
- Occupational exposures
- Allergens

## Prevention Strategies

### Common Prevention Approaches
1. **Healthy Diet**
   - Eat fruits and vegetables daily
   - Limit salt, sugar, and saturated fats
   - Choose whole grains over refined grains

2. **Physical Activity**
   - At least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
   - Include muscle-strengthening activities
   - Limit sedentary time

3. **Avoid Tobacco**
   - No smoking in any form
   - Avoid secondhand smoke
   - Seek help to quit if needed

4. **Limit Alcohol**
   - Moderate consumption only
   - Avoid binge drinking
   - Consider abstaining for health benefits

5. **Regular Check-ups**
   - Annual health screenings
   - Blood pressure monitoring
   - Blood sugar and cholesterol tests
   - Cancer screenings as recommended

## Global Impact

### Economic Burden
- NCDs cause 70% of all deaths globally
- $47 trillion in cumulative economic losses by 2030
- Disproportionate impact on low-income countries

### Social Impact
- Reduced productivity
- Increased healthcare costs
- Family caregiving burden
- Reduced quality of life
